# Gatekeeper Review: GTM Deck — Mobile Funnel
**Date:** 2026-06-07
**Reviewer:** Gatekeeper
**Verdict:** APPROVED WITH TWO NOTES

---

## Overall Assessment

This deck is strong. The data story is sharp, the positioning reframe is correct, the structure holds, and the voice is largely clean. Two items need attention before the leadership presentation. Neither is a structural problem — both are specific fixes.

---

## What Works

- **Slide 1 is the best slide in the deck.** The core tension ("Most people decide to build a website on their phone. We make them finish on a computer.") is exactly the right opening. Sharp, human, specific. The data backs it immediately.
- **The counter-intuitive insight holds its position.** "This is not an audience quality problem" on Slide 1, and the premium rate signal on Slide 3, do the right strategic work. They pre-empt the obvious leadership objection.
- **Slide 6 messaging is clean.** The headline lands. The three value props are specific, not generic. The banned word list is a useful discipline signal for the team.
- **Slide 9 risks are honest.** The "generate but not edit" risk is surfaced correctly. The biggest open question at the bottom is the right note to end on.
- **No feedback-system violations.** The AI/human framing throughout is correct — AI generates, user owns, no sequential-handoff language anywhere.

---

## Two Notes

### Note 1: Slide 2 — em dash usage (voice-dna conflict)

The line "For the first time, users can generate a complete Wix site directly from their phone — no desktop required to get started." uses an em dash. The agent instructions say never use em dashes or en dashes. Fix: replace with a period or colon.

Suggested revision:
"For the first time, users can generate a complete Wix site directly from their phone. No desktop required."

The second sentence is stronger as a standalone fragment anyway. Matches the voice-dna short-punch pattern.

---

### Note 2: Slide 8 — metric target language is soft

"Meaningful movement toward double digits" is not a target. It is a hope. Leadership will push on this. An internal deck needs to name a number, even if the number is directional.

The data brief sets the baseline at 2.0% (Harmony mobile). A defensible 60-day target is something specific: 6%, 8%, 10%. Pick one and own it. If the team is uncertain, the slide should say "Target: TBD — to be set at launch based on first 7-day baseline" rather than "meaningful movement."

Fix: replace "meaningful movement toward double digits" with a specific number or an explicit placeholder with a rationale.

## Verdict

**APPROVED** for use as the planning deck. The two notes above should be fixed before the leadership presentation, but they do not block the team from working from this draft now.

The biggest open question on Slide 9 — drop-off data at the device-switch step — is the highest-priority pre-work item before leadership review. If that number exists, it belongs on Slide 1.

## Slide 1: The Problem / Market Moment

**The core tension:**
Most people decide to build a website on their phone. We make them finish on a computer. That gap is where intent dies.

**The numbers:**
- 74% of homepage visitors arrive on mobile. Only 4% sign up. Desktop converts at 34%.
- On our AI creation page (Harmony): 66% of traffic is mobile, growing +31% M/M. Mobile conversion: 2%. Desktop: 17.9%. A 9x gap.
- The pattern is consistent across every key page. Mobile dominates visits. Desktop dominates conversions.

**This is not an audience quality problem.**
Mobile users who do convert have a higher premium rate than desktop (5.6% vs 4.2%). The intent is there. The product stops them.

**The cost of inaction:**
We are growing mobile traffic aggressively. The conversion gap is not closing on its own. Every month we wait, we are acquiring mobile visitors and converting almost none of them.

---

## Slide 2: What We Built

**One sentence:**
For the first time, users can generate a complete Wix site directly from their phone — no desktop required to get started.

**What it does:**
- User lands on mobile, enters a prompt or answers a few questions
- Wix AI generates a full site: structure, design, content
- Site is ready to view, share, and publish from mobile
- Editing remains on desktop (for now)

**What changes:**
Before: mobile visitors hit a wall. Account creation on mobile, site creation on desktop. The moment of intent gets split across two sessions, two devices, and often two days. Most people never come back.

After: the full creation moment — from "I want a site" to "I have a site" — happens on the device where the decision was made.

## Slide 3: Who It's For

**Primary segment:**
Mobile-first small business owners and creators who get the impulse to build a site while they're out living their business — between appointments, at a market, on a commute — not sitting at a desk.

**Their situation:**
- Their goal: a professional online presence, fast
- Their pain: every tool that could help them requires a device they don't have in their hand right now
- How this solves it: the gap between "I need a website" and "I have a website" collapses to minutes, on the device they're already holding

**The signal in the data:**
These users already find us. 74% of homepage visits are mobile. 66% of Harmony visits are mobile and growing. They have intent. We have been making them wait.

**Out of scope (this launch):**
Full site editing from mobile. The creation flow is the unlock. Editing is a subsequent phase.

**Our defensible position:**

## Slide 5: The GTM Strategy

**Launch motion:** Three motions in parallel. Acquisition-led, with re-engagement and brand layers running alongside.

**Channel plan:**

| Channel | Role in Launch | Timing | Owner |
|---------|---------------|--------|-------|
| Paid social (Instagram Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts) | Primary acquisition. Show the 60-second creation experience. Demo-able in 15 seconds. | Launch + 30 days | Performance marketing |
| Email + push (lapsed users, never-created segment) | Re-engagement. "The blocker is gone. Build your site now." | Launch week | CRM / Lifecycle |
| Organic social + creator seeding | Brand layer. Seed the insight: "your website moment is now on your phone." | Pre-launch + ongoing | Brand / Social |
| App store optimization | Capture intent from users searching for mobile site builders. | Pre-launch | Growth / ASO |
| PR + product page update | Launch moment. Press, wix.com/harmony update, homepage callout. | Launch day | Comms + Web |

**Sequencing logic:**
Creator seeding and ASO start before launch to warm the channel. Paid social launches simultaneously with the product go-live — the feature needs to be live before we drive traffic. Re-engagement email goes out within 48 hours of launch while the moment is fresh. Brand organic is always-on from launch forward.

## Slide 9: Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Mobile-generated site quality is below the bar | Medium | High | Hard gate: do not launch paid acquisition until quality is confirmed by internal review and user testing. Launch to organic first. |
| "Generate but not edit" frustrates users who want to customize immediately | Medium | Medium | Set expectation clearly in the creation flow: "Your site is ready. Full editing on desktop." Frame it as a preview, not a finished product. |
| Competitors ship mobile creation within 6 months, neutralizing the advantage | Medium | Medium | Ship fast, capture the narrative first. Being second in a feature is survivable. Being first and known as first matters more. |
| Paid social creative underperforms (demo video doesn't convert) | Medium | Medium | Test 3+ creative angles in the first 2 weeks before scaling spend. Demo-format, testimonial-format, and problem-framing format. |
| Lapsed user segment unsubscribe rate spikes | Low | Low | Segment carefully (users who created account in last 12 months, never built a site). One send, not a drip. |

**Biggest open question:**
Do we have data on drop-off at the device-switch step specifically? If we can quantify how many users abandon between "account created on mobile" and "site started on desktop," that number becomes the single strongest slide in this deck. Pull it before the leadership presentation.

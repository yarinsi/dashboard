# Research Summary: Bring Site to Wix
**Any2Wix · June 2026**

---

## Why People Want to Leave Their Current Platform

Users across platforms who are dissatisfied cluster around three core pain points:

**Financial frustration.** What starts as a cheap, simple site turns expensive over time. Renewal price jumps, rising platform fees, and the accumulating cost of add-ons create a gap between initial expectation and actual spend. The compounding nature of the cost — not a single moment — is what triggers action.

**Outgrowing the offering.** Users hit capability ceilings. A missing feature or a platform limit blocks them from doing something their business actually needs — accepting payments in multiple currencies, expanding design control, or scaling beyond what the tool was built for. The platform stops being a solution and starts being a constraint.

**Maintenance burden.** Keeping the site current requires more time and expertise than users anticipated. Updates are hard. Changes are complicated. The site becomes a liability instead of an asset.

---

## What's Stopping Them from Actually Switching

The gap isn't motivation — it's confidence. Users want to leave but can't make the move feel safe.

Their concerns fall into two layers:

**The migration process itself** feels risky and costly. Rebuilding takes time. Executing correctly often requires technical skill — or hiring someone who has it. And throughout, the site might go down. The combination of effort, cost, and risk of downtime creates paralysis. Users end up staying on a platform they dislike because the cost of leaving feels higher than the cost of staying.

**The end result feels uncertain**, especially across two areas that matter most:

*SEO and site authority.* Users who've spent months or years building search rankings fear losing them. Redirects, canonical tags, and link equity are hard to preserve — and hard to understand. The fear isn't just losing traffic; it's undoing years of work.

*Business and content data.* Users with product catalogs, customer lists, and order histories fear the data won't survive the move. For many, this is the single biggest blocker. The platform is tolerated because the data is trapped.

---

## The Vibe-Code Platform Angle

Sites built on vibe-code platforms (Lovable, Bolt, etc.) present a specific and growing migration opportunity. These users didn't fail to launch — they launched, then hit new walls:

**Sites aren't crawlable.** Vibe-code tools build with JavaScript by default. Search engines receive an empty HTML shell, not real content. Getting indexed requires manual, technical SEO work that most users don't know how to do and didn't expect to need.

**Credit ceilings create friction.** Users start excited, then become conservative with changes because every iteration costs money. The creative loop — the core value proposition of vibe-coding — breaks down when experimentation feels expensive.

**Design homogeneity.** Generated UI tends to look the same across sites. Fine-tuning via prompt has limits. Users who want a distinctive look hit a ceiling the tool can't clear.

---

## The Emerging Migration Services Market

A market has already formed around moving people off vibe-code platforms — signaling real, unmet demand:

- **Gig economy:** Freelancers on platforms like Fiverr offering dedicated vibe-code-to-builder migration services
- **Agency services:** WordPress agencies adding specific service lines for migrating sites from Lovable, Bolt, and similar tools
- **Dedicated products:** Purpose-built platforms (e.g., lovableconverter.com) that translate and export vibe-code output for import into traditional builders

Most of these solutions involve exporting and converting source code — an approach that doesn't map directly to Harmony's architecture. This is both a constraint and a positioning opportunity.

---

## Summary Signal

The research points to one consistent truth: **the problem is not wanting to switch. The problem is not being able to trust the switch.**

The highest-leverage messaging angle is confidence — in the process (fast, safe, no downtime) and in the outcome (rankings protected, data intact, design that's actually yours).

Subject: A/B Test Results: [HP EN] Domain Search Bar Above the Header

Hi Everyone,

**TL;DR: After running the test for 7 days, we don't see a significant impact on sign-ups or premiums so we reverted to A and will work on a new iteration.**

**Main KPIs:**
- Sign-up success - non-significant, -0.17% stable (A: 8.8% 32,531 | B: 8.8% 32,806). On mobile there's a negative trend (-1.43%, -85, non-sig), likely caused by fewer clicks on the email field in the hero (-8.2%, sig, -552). The search bar above the fold appears to compete with the primary conversion path on small screens.

**Additional KPIs:**
- Domain search bar engagement - significant positive on both desktop (+103%, +519) and mobile (+18%, +106). Users with domain intent engaged with the entry point when it was prominent above the fold. The hypothesis held.
- Above-the-fold attention - more clicks on the main CTA on desktop (+7.85%, sig, +550) and less scroll below the hero. B captured more attention in the top section, but it didn't translate to signups.

**Insights:**
Version B confirmed that a high-visibility domain entry point drives domain search engagement. That said, we expected to see even more impact, and suspect the current position and design of the search bar aren't prominent or clear enough. That will be addressed in the next iteration.

On mobile, the search bar pulls attention away from the email field in the hero, creating a leak in the signup flow. This isn't treated in the next iteration.

~370K anons were exposed in each group.
